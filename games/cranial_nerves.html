Eye Movement Nerves (III, IV, VI)
"LR₆SO₄ — the rest are III"

LR6 = Lateral Rectus → CN VI (Abducens)
SO4 = Superior Oblique → CN IV (Trochlear)
Rest = all other eye muscles → CN III (Oculomotor)

CN V vs CN VII — Common Confusion
"Five Feels, Seven Smiles"

CN V (Trigeminal) = sensation of the face (you FEEL with 5 fingers)
CN VII (Facial) = movement of the face (smiling uses 7 muscles — close enough!)

Bell's palsy = CN VII (can't move face)
Trigeminal neuralgia = CN V (excruciating facial pain)

CN X (Vagus) — The Wanderer
"Vagus Wanders Everywhere"

Vagus = Latin for "wandering." CN X travels further than any other cranial nerve — from brainstem all the way to the abdomen.

Heart → slows rate (parasympathetic)
Lungs → bronchoconstriction
GI → increases motility
Throat → swallowing & gag reflex

Pure Sensory Nerves (I, II, VIII)
"1, 2, 8 — Just Sense, Don't Wait"

Only three cranial nerves are purely sensory:

CN I — Olfactory (smell)
CN II — Optic (vision)
CN VIII — Vestibulocochlear (hearing & balance)

All others have at least some motor component.
